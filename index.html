My Projects

Malware Classification using ML models

Most of us, at least once, might have experienced a malware threat to your software services. How would the effect be when you are targeted and experiences back-to-back cyber attacks. This project uses data from such a hypothetical survivor to classify the different classes of malwares to help individuals and organizations to recognize a malware faster and act quickly. This project used several ML models for the purpose and compared their accuracies. Random Forest Classifier gave an impressive 94.3% accuracy on train data, but only 69.6% on test data implying a possible overfitting!
PCA, Linear SVM, radial-based SVM, Random Forest, LDA, QDA, K-Means

Mall Customer Segmentation using K-Means Clustering

Ever wondered how to segment your customers who visit and shop at the shopping mall. This customer segmentation model is for you! Many of your assumptions will be right, yes, high-earning, youngsters are top spenders as you expected! The Analysis also shows that the highest number of customers are those in their 30s who visit the mall.
Python libraries, EDA, Univariate and Bivariate Analysis, K-Means

Image Classification for Breast Cancer Detection

This Deep Learning project gives the probability of a sample image being cancerous or benign. Cancer detection by analyzing the scan images of organs is still considered a manual skill. The inaccuracies that could result in wrong diagnosis is intolerable in medical field. This project attempted to classify the sample images into different probability classes rather than stating benign or malignant. This could help the practitioners get a secondary opinion on their judgement in a quantitative measure. It was implemented using 3 CNN, 3 Linear, 1 ReLU, 1 Sigmoid, and 1 Max Pooling layers.
PyTorch, Sci-Kit Learn, OpenCV

Steam Review Analysis using Apache Spark and Hadoop

Dedicated to all the Gamers out there! Not sure which game to go for next? Why not take a look at the most popular and highly recommended games on the Steam platform. And if you are a newbie to gaming, don't worry the ALS algorithm's got your back!
This analysis project dealt with a data of over 7GB which was accessed using HDFS and analyzed using Spark SQL in the Jupyter Notebook. We derived numerous interesting trends in gaming in the year of 2021 and no surprise, PUBG won the competition!
PySpark (SQL and ML library), HDFS, Python libraries

Analyzing & Visualizing the Motor Accidents in Maryland Counties

Maryland faces a huge loss of life and property due to motor accidents annually. As a driver in the state, I was conscious about this issue. The data scientist mindset insisted in developing an analysis of the accident trends which could be used for safety measures one day. Using the ggplot and plot packages in RStudio, the project successfully developed an interactive map of Maryland showing the accident spots on the Highways. With further statistical modeling, the accident spots can be narrowed down and sharpened to spots to be added to live map applications or road signs to warn the drivers.
RStudio (R), GitHub, Quarto, RevealJS

Live Location Tracker of ISS using Folium and Requests

How cool is it to track something like the International Space Station? This project utilized the live coordinates provided by NASA to locate the ISS for an hour. It is pretty amazing to understand how fast the ISS moves as it covers more than half of the planet's circumference in an hour! The live tracking was mapped in HTML on a webpage using the Folium library. The data extraction was done by web scrapping the NASA website using the Requests library. This project is a wake up call to understanding the power of data!
Folium, Requests HDFS, Python libraries

About Me

Insightful Data Science graduate student with immense technical and human potential to bring the best in every situation. The prowess of mathematics and the curiosity of a Physicist combined with the enthusiasm to work and maintain an organized and balanced life keeps me on top of the game. Before focusing on a Data Science career, I graduated from Pondicherry University, India with a Master's in Physics. Yes, then why DS? Honestly, this is the question I have faced the most and the hardest to answer! To begin with I realized the power of Data during my Undergraduate internship at Cochin University of Science and Technology (CUSAT). I got the opportunity to work ...with data on different weather parameters to understand the El-Nino Southern Oscillations and their effect on the Indian monsoon.

This work inspired me to undertake new data analysis projects. In the final year of my first Master's degree, I conducted an extensive data analysis on the Internet Infrastructure in India - pre and post-COVID-19. As a part of this project, I collected data from various open sources in the form of CSV, JSON, and web scraping, did data cleaning, EDA, and data visualization. Moreover, I collected a set of primary data through a survey. In this process, I discovered numerous resources, platforms, technologies, and courses, and received professional certification from IBM in Data Science. I learned Python, SQL, and MS Excel during this project.

From then, I made sure that I kept up with my skills and decided to pursue a Master's in Data Science - to learn from the experts and to get more expertise in the new field. Throughout the current program, I undertook numerous projects that helped me hone my technical and management skills. Today, I look forward to establishing a strong base in the field and offering the best from my expertise.
